    <div class="card"><h3 id="what-title">WHAT IS PRODUCE?</h3><p id="what-text">Building on the legacy of the previous LGBTIQ+ Screens project, PRODUCE is a research project funded by the Spanish Ministry of Science, Innovation and Universities that moves beyond the study of representation to explore the very engine of audiovisual content: the creators and production contexts. Between 2026 and 2029, this project will investigate how audiovisual content about LGBTIQ+ people is produced in Spain across a rich ecosystem of cultural media – from cinema and television to social media, independent creation, and activism. PRODUCE seeks to understand how production dynamics shape not only LGBTIQ+ content, but also the realities of those who create it, and ultimately how such content can reduce prejudice and foster a more inclusive society.</p></div>

    <div class="card"><h3 id="whatdo-title">WHAT DO WE DO?</h3><p id="whatdo-text">PRODUCE investigates the production dynamics of LGBTIQ+ content, understanding how production contexts and intersectional variables such as age, gender, ethnicity, and class of professionals, amateurs, activists, and online producers influence production routines, creative processes, perceived professional value, well‑being, and the resulting LGBTIQ+ representations. We will also identify emerging roles (i.e. LGBTIQ+ authenticity consultants and intimacy coordinators), opportunities and tensions in the industry, studying how current media structures and new cultural practices affect creative self‑esteem, agency, and the potential for LGBTIQ+ prejudice reduction.</p></div>

    <div class="card"><h3 id="outcomes-title">EXPECTED OUTCOMES</h3><p id="outcomes-text">PRODUCE aims at mapping how production dynamics shape LGBTIQ+ audiovisual content in the Spanish state. Our research will be disseminated through peer reviewed publications, training courses for university education, best practice manuals for the audiovisual industry, and two international congresses on media, gender and sexuality.</p></div>
